Common Reporting Outcome Standards Schema and Working Architecture for Legible, Knowledge-Ready Intake  ·  CC0  ·  github.com/cross-walkri

Round design and form field design are where governance decisions meet the road. CROSS specifies what grantees must demonstrate at each payment gate, declared before any applicant sees the form. WALKRI specifies the quality of the fields through which that demonstration is collected. Select your role to find prompts built for your specific situation.

Across the 95+ frameworks documented in this corpus, from the Hewlett Foundation's evaluation approach to USAID PIRS to OECD DAC criteria, a consistent pattern emerges: data quality problems in grant programs trace to specification decisions made before the first application was submitted. Round design and form field design are where funding governance either happens with intention or happens by default.

A funder who treats these as administrative overhead is making governance decisions without acknowledging that governance decisions are being made. The field that does not specify what it measures is not neutral: it transfers interpretive power to whoever scores it, invisibly, after submission. CROSS+WALKRI addresses this at the source, before any applicant sees the form.
