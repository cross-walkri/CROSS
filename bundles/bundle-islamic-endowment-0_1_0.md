# CROSS+WALKRI Program Bundle: Islamic and Endowment-Based Giving

---

## Overview

This bundle covers grant and funding programs that operate under Islamic jurisprudential frameworks, including obligatory almsgiving mechanisms, perpetual endowment structures, voluntary charitable giving, and multilateral development financing from an Islamic perspective. The ecosystem includes Zakat distribution programs, Waqf endowment grants, Sadaqah voluntary giving, and Islamic Development Bank affiliated programs.

Four program sub-types operate in this space with materially different obligation structures, jurisprudential constraints, and accountability requirements. This bundle addresses all four and notes where provisions diverge.

**Sub-type A: Zakat distribution programs** distribute the obligatory annual almsgiving (2.5% of nisab-exceeding wealth) to recipients within the eight categories enumerated in Quran 9:60 (fuqara, masakin, amilin, muallafat qulubuhum, riqab, gharimin, fi sabilillah, ibn sabil). Eligibility is jurisprudentially pre-specified; the entry gate's primary function is verifying that recipients fall within eligible categories.

**Sub-type B: Waqf endowment grants** distribute income generated by a perpetual Islamic endowment. The Waqf corpus (principal) is held in perpetuity; only investment income or usufruct may be distributed. This perpetuity constraint is the defining accountability structure of Waqf grantmaking and imposes obligations with no secular parallel.

**Sub-type C: Sadaqah and voluntary giving programs** distribute non-obligatory charitable contributions. These programs lack the structural eligibility constraints of Zakat and the perpetuity constraints of Waqf, but still require outcome specification and Theory of Change documentation under CROSS+WALKRI.

**Sub-type D: Islamic Development Bank and IsDB-affiliated grant programs** operate under multilateral development financing principles from an Islamic perspective, applying OECD DAC-equivalent evaluation criteria (relevance, effectiveness, efficiency, impact, sustainability) alongside Shariah compliance requirements.

## Part 1: CROSS Runbook

### Obligation Mode Selection

**Sub-type A (Zakat distribution):** Use Change obligation mode. Zakat is designed to improve the condition of eligible recipients; the obligation is fulfilled when the recipient's condition improves within the jurisprudentially recognized eligible categories. The entry gate's primary criterion is eligibility verification: confirming that each recipient falls within one of the eight Quranic categories and holds wealth below the nisab threshold.

**Sub-type B (Waqf endowment grants):** Use Change obligation mode for the grant programs funded by Waqf income. The distinctive feature is a mandatory sustainability constraint at the entry gate: the waqfiyya deed governs what beneficiary classes are eligible, and the Waqf corpus preservation obligation governs what evidence is required at the completion gate. The corpus principal must not be depleted; the completion gate must include documentation confirming this.

**Sub-type C (Sadaqah programs):** Use Build or Change obligation mode depending on whether the program funds a specific output or a measurable condition change. Sadaqah programs have the most flexible obligation structure in this bundle; CROSS's standard Build and Change provisions apply without modification.

**Sub-type D (IsDB-affiliated programs):** Use Change obligation mode aligned with the IsDB Independent Evaluation Department framework. The IsDB IEvD evaluation criteria (relevance, effectiveness, efficiency, impact, sustainability) map to CROSS's Theory of Change hierarchy with Maqasid al-Shariah as the goal tier.

### Entry Specification Gate

Before any application window opens, the following must be committed to in writing and published in a verifiable location (program documentation, institutional governance record, or published program guidelines with a date-stamped version):

**Program specification:**
- Obligation mode (Build, Change, or Retroactive)
- Total funding pool size and currency denomination
- Eligible recipient categories (for Zakat: which of the eight Quranic categories; for Waqf: beneficiary class specified in the waqfiyya deed; for Sadaqah: declared beneficiary population)
- Eligibility criteria including any nisab threshold verification requirements (Sub-type A)
- Theory of Change goal tier mapped to Maqasid al-Shariah objectives (for Sub-types A, B, D)
- Shariah compliance authority: which scholar or board certifies compliance and what the certification covers
- AAOIFI standard reference: which specific AAOIFI standard governs this program (Standard 35 for Zakat, Standard 33 for Waqf)

**Waqf corpus preservation declaration (Sub-type B):** Programs funded by Waqf income must declare at the entry gate: how the Waqf corpus is protected throughout the grant period, which institutional arrangement holds and manages the corpus, and what audit or review mechanism confirms that only income (not principal) is distributed. This declaration is part of the entry specification gate, not deferred to the completion gate.

**Population scope declaration:** The eight eligible Zakat categories constitute the most historically precedented pre-specified population constraint in any giving tradition. For Zakat distribution programs, the population scope declaration maps to a Zakat category eligibility declaration. Each category included in the program scope must be named at the entry gate with its operational definition for this program context.

**Nisab threshold verification method:** Before any recipient is enrolled, the program must declare what documentation confirms recipient eligibility below the nisab threshold. This is a WALKRI compliance threshold that must be specified at the entry gate; post-hoc verification methods are not acceptable under CROSS's entry specification gate requirement.

**Shariah compliance as an evaluation criterion:** Faith-mission alignment in Islamic programs is a formal evaluation criterion with no secular parallel. The program must specify at the entry gate what evidence constitutes Shariah compliance verification, which body or scholar certifies compliance, and what the certification scope covers. This is not a procedural formality; it is a substantive evaluation criterion that governs the completion gate.

### Application Gate

**Recipient eligibility verification (Sub-type A):** Each application or enrollment must document: which of the eight Quranic categories the recipient belongs to, what documentation confirms wealth below the nisab threshold, and which verification method the program applies. Verification methods must match the method declared at the entry specification gate.

**Waqf beneficiary class verification (Sub-type B):** Each application must confirm that the proposed use falls within the beneficiary class specified in the waqfiyya deed. Applications proposing uses outside the deed's beneficiary class cannot be approved regardless of merit; the waqfiyya deed governs the scope with legal authority.

**Sadaqah purpose declaration (Sub-type C):** Each application must declare what condition the gift addresses and what outcome the program aims to produce. The absence of Shariah eligibility constraints does not exempt Sadaqah programs from outcome specification; CROSS requires outcome specification for all programs regardless of the mechanism that funds them.

**Maqasid al-Shariah alignment (Sub-types A, B, D):** Applications must declare which of the five Maqasid objectives the program addresses (preservation of life, intellect, lineage, property, or religion) and by what measure the program's contribution to that objective will be assessed. The Maqasid objectives function as the goal tier in CROSS's Theory of Change hierarchy for Islamic development programs.

**IsDB IEvD alignment (Sub-type D):** Applications must address relevance, effectiveness, efficiency, impact, and sustainability per the IEvD framework. These map directly to OECD DAC criteria in the Islamic development context; programs already familiar with DAC-aligned application requirements will find the mapping straightforward.

### Completion Gate

**Sub-type A (Zakat):** Completion evidence must confirm: funds were distributed to recipients within the declared eligible categories, nisab threshold verification was applied to each recipient per the method declared at the entry gate, and no funds were distributed to recipients outside the declared eligible categories. The eight eligible categories are a closed set; distributions outside them constitute a completion gate failure regardless of the humanitarian merits of the recipients.

**Sub-type B (Waqf):** Completion evidence must include, in addition to standard program outcome documentation: confirmation that the Waqf corpus principal was not depleted during the grant period. This confirmation must come from an independent source (auditor, Waqf administrator, or institutional Shariah board) and must address the corpus balance at the beginning and end of the grant period. A completion gate assessment that does not include corpus preservation confirmation does not satisfy this bundle's requirements.

**Sub-type C (Sadaqah):** Standard CROSS completion gate provisions apply. Evidence must demonstrate that the condition declared at the entry gate was addressed by the program's activities.

**Sub-type D (IsDB):** Completion evidence addresses the IEvD five criteria. The sustainability criterion requires forward-looking evidence: what mechanisms will sustain program outcomes after grant funding ends.

**Shariah compliance confirmation:** For Sub-types A, B, and D, the completion gate must include a statement from the designated Shariah compliance authority confirming that program execution remained within the scope certified at the entry gate. Mid-grant Shariah compliance incidents must be disclosed and addressed before the completion gate assessment.

### Attestation Gate and Corpus

The Attestation Corpus for Islamic and endowment-based programs must address the institutional specificity of these grant types:

- Program specification record: published program guidelines with AAOIFI standard reference and Shariah compliance authority identified, date-stamped before the program opens
- Recipient eligibility records (Sub-type A): documentation of nisab threshold verification for each enrolled recipient, maintained in a form consistent with the institution's Shariah board requirements
- Waqf corpus records (Sub-type B): audited beginning and ending corpus balance for each grant period, linked to the program record in the attestation corpus
- Completion gate determination: recorded by the program authority with Shariah compliance confirmation attached
- Maqasid outcome documentation: linked to the Theory of Change goal tier declared at the entry gate

For IsDB-affiliated programs, the IEvD evaluation report constitutes the completion and attestation record.

### Continuation Gate

For recurring programs:

A continuing Zakat distribution program must demonstrate: all prior distributions fell within declared eligible categories, nisab verification methods remain consistent with the current entry specification, and the nisab threshold (which changes with gold and silver prices) has been updated to its current value before the new distribution period opens.

A continuing Waqf endowment program must demonstrate: corpus principal preservation throughout all prior grant periods (audited), income available for the current period calculated from current corpus valuation, and beneficiary class scope confirmed against the current waqfiyya deed (accounting for any judicial modifications to the deed).

## Part 2: WALKRI Field Specifications

The following field specifications cover the intake fields most commonly required in Islamic and endowment-based grant programs. Each specification satisfies WALKRI's five pre-publication requirements: criterion intent, operational definition, response form, evidence form, compliance threshold.

---

### Field: Eligible Recipient Category

**Criterion intent:** Establishes which of the jurisprudentially recognized eligible categories the recipient belongs to, providing the primary gate criterion for Zakat distribution and the scope constraint for Waqf beneficiary enrollment.

**Operational definition:** For Zakat distribution programs: the eligible recipient category is one of the eight categories enumerated in Quran 9:60 (fuqara: the poor; masakin: the destitute; amilin: Zakat administrators; muallafat qulubuhum: those whose hearts are to be reconciled; riqab: those in bondage; gharimin: those in debt; fi sabilillah: those in the way of God; ibn sabil: wayfarers). For Waqf grant programs: the eligible beneficiary class is the class specified in the waqfiyya deed establishing this particular Waqf; the deed is the governing document and its language controls. Programs may not extend the beneficiary class beyond the deed's specification without judicial authorization.

**Response form:** Selection of the applicable category from the declared eligible categories for this program, with a brief description of how the applicant or recipient fits the category definition. For Waqf programs: a statement that the proposed use falls within the beneficiary class specified in the waqfiyya deed, with a citation to the relevant deed provision.

**Evidence form:** For Zakat programs: documentation of recipient condition consistent with the category definition (income documentation, debt records, or equivalent as appropriate to the category). For Waqf programs: a copy of or citation to the relevant waqfiyya deed provision, confirmed by the Waqf administrator. The institutional Shariah board may specify additional evidence requirements; those requirements must be incorporated into the entry specification gate if they apply universally.

**Compliance threshold:** The recipient's documented condition must match the definition of the declared eligible category. A recipient who does not fall within any of the eight Quranic categories does not satisfy this field for Zakat programs, regardless of financial need. For Waqf programs: a proposed use that falls outside the waqfiyya deed's beneficiary class does not satisfy this field, regardless of merit.

---

### Field: Nisab Threshold Verification

**Criterion intent:** Confirms that each Zakat distribution recipient holds wealth below the nisab threshold, which is the Shariah-prescribed wealth level below which a Muslim is eligible to receive Zakat rather than obligated to pay it.

**Operational definition:** The nisab threshold is the minimum wealth level above which Zakat becomes obligatory. It is calculated based on the value of 85 grams of gold or 595 grams of silver, whichever produces the lower threshold (this is the standard applied by most contemporary Zakat institutions, though madhab variation exists). A recipient eligible for Zakat holds net assets below the applicable nisab threshold. The program must declare at the entry gate which nisab calculation methodology it applies and what the current threshold is in the program's operating currency as of the program opening date.

**Response form:** A declaration by the applicant or verified by the distributing institution that the recipient's net assets fall below the declared nisab threshold, with the applicable threshold value stated in the program's operating currency. For institutional Zakat programs that conduct in-person needs assessment: the verification officer's confirmation serves as the response form.

**Evidence form:** Income and asset documentation appropriate to the jurisdiction and program context: tax records, bank statements, income declarations, or institutional needs assessment records. For programs operating in low-documentation contexts, the institution's Shariah board may authorize alternative verification methods; those methods must be specified at the entry gate. Self-declaration is not sufficient as the sole evidence form for programs holding AAOIFI Standard 35 compliance.

**Compliance threshold:** The recipient's documented net assets must fall below the nisab threshold as calculated by the declared methodology as of the distribution date. Recipients whose assets exceed the nisab threshold are not eligible for Zakat distribution; no exceptions are recognized by AAOIFI Standard 35. Programs using the silver nisab (typically lower) must apply that threshold consistently throughout the distribution period.

---

### Field: Shariah Compliance Certification

**Criterion intent:** Documents that the program design, execution, and outcomes fall within the scope of Shariah as certified by a qualified authority, providing the formal faith-mission compliance evaluation for Islamic grant programs.

**Operational definition:** Shariah compliance certification is the formal determination by a qualified Islamic scholar or institutional Shariah supervisory board that the program's structure, activities, and fund flows conform to Islamic jurisprudence as applicable to the program type (Zakat, Waqf, Sadaqah, or Islamic development finance). The certifying authority must be identified by name and institutional affiliation. The certification scope must be specified: what aspects of the program the certification covers (fund sourcing, distribution methodology, recipient eligibility, investment of corpus funds if applicable) and what aspects are outside the certification scope.

**Response form:** A named certifying authority (individual scholar with recognized qualification or institutional Shariah supervisory board), the certification scope described in the terms used by the certifying authority, the date of certification, and the period the certification covers. For AAOIFI-member institutions: the applicable AAOIFI standard reference (Standard 35 for Zakat, Standard 33 for Waqf) and confirmation that the program structure is assessed against that standard.

**Evidence form:** A written certification or fatwa from the named authority, or the institutional Shariah board's published resolution covering this program type. For AAOIFI-member institutions: the annual Shariah audit report covering the relevant program. The certification document must predate the program opening; a certification issued after a distribution period opens does not satisfy the entry specification gate requirement.

**Compliance threshold:** A named certifying authority, a defined certification scope, and a dated certification document that predates the program opening. An unnamed "Shariah board" without institutional identification does not satisfy this field. A certification covering only the fund sourcing (not the distribution methodology) does not satisfy this field for Zakat distribution programs. Programs that cannot identify a certifying authority before the program opens must defer the program opening until certification is obtained.

---

### Field: Waqf Corpus Preservation Evidence

**Criterion intent:** Confirms that the Waqf principal (the original endowment corpus) was not depleted during the grant period, satisfying the perpetuity constraint that is the defining legal and Shariah obligation of Waqf grantmaking.

**Operational definition:** The Waqf corpus is the original endowment asset (cash, real property, or other asset) transferred by the waqif (endowment founder) into perpetual Waqf. The corpus must be preserved in perpetuity; only income generated by the corpus (investment returns, rental income, usufruct) may be distributed. Corpus depletion occurs when any portion of the principal is distributed, transferred, or lost to preventable causes during the grant period. Market fluctuation in corpus value (for investment-based Waqf) must be distinguished from principal distribution: unrealized losses due to market movements are not depletion in the same sense as distributions from principal, but significant unrealized losses must be disclosed and addressed in the preservation evidence.

**Response form:** An audited statement of the Waqf corpus balance at the beginning and end of the grant period, prepared by an auditor or Waqf administrator independent of the distributing program. The statement must identify the corpus components, their valuation basis, and confirm that no distributions were made from principal during the period. For real property Waqf: a property condition report and confirmation that ownership of the corpus property has not changed.

**Evidence form:** Audited financial statements covering the grant period, specifically addressing corpus balance. For AAOIFI-member institutions: the Waqf-specific financial reporting under AAOIFI Financial Accounting Standard 33. For smaller Waqf programs: a letter from the Waqf administrator attesting to corpus integrity, supported by account statements or property records. Self-reported corpus preservation without independent confirmation does not satisfy this field.

**Compliance threshold:** The corpus balance at the end of the grant period must equal or exceed the balance at the beginning of the period, adjusted for documented income reinvestment (where the waqfiyya deed permits reinvestment). A corpus that has decreased in value due to distributions from principal does not satisfy this field regardless of the beneficial use to which the distributed funds were put. Programs that discover mid-grant corpus depletion must report this to the relevant oversight authority immediately and disclose it at the completion gate.

---

### Field: Maqasid al-Shariah Outcome Mapping

**Criterion intent:** Establishes which of the five objectives of Islamic law this program addresses and by what measure the program's contribution to that objective will be assessed, providing the Theory of Change goal tier for Islamic development programs.

**Operational definition:** Maqasid al-Shariah (the higher objectives of Islamic law) are the five ends that Islamic legal rules are designed to protect and promote: preservation of life (hifz al-nafs), preservation of intellect (hifz al-aql), preservation of lineage and family (hifz al-nasl), preservation of property (hifz al-mal), and preservation of religion (hifz al-din). These objectives function as the goal tier in CROSS's Theory of Change hierarchy for Islamic programs: program outcomes (measurable changes in recipient conditions) contribute to one or more Maqasid objectives, which constitute the ultimate normative standard against which program impact is assessed. A program may address multiple objectives; each addressed objective must be mapped separately.

**Response form:** For each Maqasid objective the program addresses: the objective named in English and Arabic, a description of how program outcomes contribute to that objective, and the measure by which the program's contribution to the objective will be assessed. The measure must be specified with enough precision to enable post-program assessment. For programs addressing preservation of property: an economic indicator (income change, asset accumulation, debt reduction). For programs addressing preservation of intellect: an educational or cognitive indicator. For programs addressing preservation of life: a health or food security indicator.

**Evidence form:** At completion: outcome data linked to the measures declared in the Maqasid outcome mapping. For IsDB-affiliated programs: the IEvD impact evaluation addressing the declared Maqasid objectives. The evidence form for Maqasid outcomes does not require Shariah-specific documentation; standard social program outcome measurement methods (surveys, administrative records, third-party assessments) are appropriate.

**Compliance threshold:** Each addressed Maqasid objective must have at least one specified measure with a baseline value (or a plan to establish a baseline at program entry) and a target value or direction of change. An outcome mapping that names a Maqasid objective without a specified measure does not satisfy this field. Programs that claim to address all five objectives must specify measures for each; a general claim of comprehensive Islamic benefit without objective-specific measures does not satisfy this field.

---

### Field: Sadaqah Purpose Declaration

**Criterion intent:** Establishes what condition the voluntary charitable gift addresses and what the program aims to produce, providing the outcome specification required by CROSS for all programs regardless of the funding mechanism.

**Operational definition:** A Sadaqah purpose declaration specifies the human or social condition the voluntary gift is directed toward and the change the program intends to produce. Unlike Zakat, Sadaqah carries no Shariah-prescribed eligibility constraint on recipients; the purpose is bounded only by the donor's intent and general Shariah permissibility. However, CROSS requires outcome specification for all programs, and outcome specification requires that the condition being addressed and the intended change be stated with enough specificity to assess whether the change occurred.

**Response form:** A description of the condition the program addresses (what situation or need is present among the target population), the population the program serves (who will receive assistance), and the intended change (what will be different for that population because of the program's activities). For programs with multiple giving streams: a purpose declaration for each stream that has a distinct purpose or population.

**Evidence form:** At completion: evidence that the declared population was served and that the intended change was pursued. The evidence standard for Sadaqah programs is the same as for other Change mode programs; the voluntary nature of the gift does not reduce the evidence standard. Programs choosing not to specify outcomes at the entry gate do not satisfy CROSS's entry specification gate requirement and cannot be entered into the CROSS Obligation Fulfillment Record.

**Compliance threshold:** A stated condition, a defined population, and an intended change, each specific enough to assess at the completion gate. "Helping the poor" is not a sufficient purpose declaration; it lacks population specificity and does not name the intended change. "Reducing food insecurity among households below the poverty line in [specified geography] by providing monthly food packages to [specified number] households" satisfies this field.

---

### Field: AAOIFI Standard Reference

**Criterion intent:** Identifies which AAOIFI Shariah Standard governs this program, anchoring the program's structural requirements to the formally published standards of the Accounting and Auditing Organisation for Islamic Financial Institutions.

**Operational definition:** AAOIFI Shariah Standards are formally published standards governing Islamic financial and charitable instruments. For programs in this bundle: Shariah Standard 35 governs Zakat calculation and distribution; Shariah Standard 33 governs Waqf establishment and administration. AAOIFI membership is required for these standards to be mandatory; AAOIFI-member institutions are obligated to comply. Non-member institutions may voluntarily adopt AAOIFI standards as a reference; in that case, the adoption must be declared and the compliance scope specified.

**Response form:** The applicable AAOIFI Shariah Standard number and title, a statement of whether the institution is an AAOIFI member (making compliance mandatory) or a voluntary adopter (making compliance a declared reference point), and any known divergences between the institution's practices and the applicable standard. Divergences must be disclosed at the entry gate; divergences discovered during the grant period must be disclosed immediately.

**Evidence form:** For AAOIFI-member institutions: the current membership status and the annual Shariah audit report referencing compliance with the applicable standard. For voluntary adopters: the institution's governing board or Shariah supervisory board resolution declaring adoption of the standard as a reference.

**Compliance threshold:** A named AAOIFI standard with a membership or adoption statement. Programs that claim AAOIFI compliance without an identified standard and a membership or adoption statement do not satisfy this field. Divergences from the applicable standard that are not disclosed at the entry gate constitute a completion gate failure if discovered during assessment.

## Part 3: Compatibility Map

This section maps the Islamic and endowment-based giving program type to the frameworks in the CROSS+WALKRI compatibility corpus that are most directly applicable. The map is organized by primitive.

---

### Pre-specification Primitive

The pre-specification primitive requires that indicators, criteria, and impact metrics be committed to before any applicant submits. Islamic grant programs that implement this primitive with the highest degree of historical precedent:

- **AAOIFI Standard 35 (Zakat)**: the eight eligible categories are jurisprudentially pre-specified; no program may expand this set. The entry specification gate for Zakat programs is, in one respect, pre-populated by 14 centuries of jurisprudential tradition.
- **AAOIFI Standard 33 (Waqf)**: the waqfiyya deed pre-specifies the beneficiary class at endowment founding; the distributing program cannot modify the deed's scope.
- **IsDB IEvD evaluation criteria**: relevance, effectiveness, efficiency, impact, sustainability declared in program design documents before evaluation.
- **CROSS entry specification gate**: the canonical implementation of this primitive across all program types.

Compatibility statements: AAOIFI Shariah Standard 35, AAOIFI Shariah Standard 33, IsDB IEvD Evaluation Guidelines, OECD DAC Criteria (referenced by IsDB), GFGP ARS 1651.

---

### Population Scope Primitive

The population scope primitive requires that the eligible population be specified before applications open. The Zakat eight eligible categories constitute the most historically precedented pre-specified population constraint in any giving tradition:

- **Zakat eight eligible categories (Quran 9:60)**: the scope was set by revelation; no program authority may modify it. This is the theoretical ceiling of pre-specification in the grant corpus: a population scope that is not subject to program-level revision.
- **Waqf waqfiyya deed**: the deed's beneficiary class is the program's population scope; it may only be modified by judicial process.
- **IsDB target population declarations**: aligned with OECD DAC disaggregation requirements.

Compatibility statements: AAOIFI Standard 35, OECD DAC (disaggregation equivalent), INPAS (restricted fund treatment).

---

### Sustainability Stance Primitive

The sustainability stance primitive requires that programs declare their approach to maintaining outcomes after the grant period. The Waqf perpetuity constraint is the most stringent sustainability requirement in the CROSS+WALKRI corpus:

- **Waqf corpus preservation**: the corpus must be preserved not just for the grant period but in perpetuity. This exceeds any secular framework's sustainability requirement by orders of magnitude.
- **IsDB sustainability criterion**: forward-looking evidence of post-grant sustainability aligned with IEvD criteria.
- **USCCB/CCHD faith-mission alignment criterion**: the closest secular-context parallel to Shariah compliance as a sustainability dimension (a program that drifts from its faith-mission basis has failed a sustainability criterion even if its outputs continue).

Compatibility statements: AAOIFI Standard 33 (Waqf perpetuity), IsDB IEvD Guidelines (sustainability criterion), USCCB/CCHD Faith-Mission Alignment (parallel faith-mission constraint), INPAS (restricted fund classification for corpus).

---

### Faith-Mission Compliance Threshold Primitive

The faith-mission compliance threshold primitive requires programs anchored in religious identity to specify what evidence constitutes compliance with that identity:

- **AAOIFI Shariah certification**: the most formally institutionalized faith-mission compliance threshold in the grant corpus, with published standards, membership obligations, and annual audit requirements.
- **IsDB Shariah governance**: multilateral institutional Shariah compliance with IDB Group Shariah Board oversight.
- **USCCB/CCHD faith-mission alignment**: the closest parallel in Catholic-identity philanthropy; specifies what organizational alignment with Catholic social teaching constitutes.

Compatibility statements: AAOIFI Shariah Standards, USCCB/CCHD Grant Program Guidelines, IsDB IDB Group Shariah Board.

---

### Causality Stance Primitive

The causality stance primitive requires programs to declare the causal mechanism by which activities produce outcomes. The Maqasid al-Shariah framework provides a five-objective goal tier for Islamic programs:

- **Maqasid al-Shariah (five objectives)**: a Theory of Change framework embedded in Islamic jurisprudence. Programs address one or more objectives; activities produce outcomes that contribute to the relevant objective. This is the goal tier in CROSS's Theory of Change hierarchy.
- **IsDB IEvD effectiveness criterion**: causal assessment of whether program activities produced declared outcomes.
- **OECD DAC impact criterion**: the long-run causal link between program outcomes and development goals; used by IsDB as the equivalent framework.

Compatibility statements: IsDB IEvD Evaluation Guidelines, OECD DAC Impact Criterion, GFGP ARS 1651 (organizational Theory of Change capacity).

---

### Organizational Identity Primitive

The organizational identity primitive requires programs to declare the legal, governance, and accountability identity of the applying entity:

- **AAOIFI governance standards**: institutional governance requirements for AAOIFI-member institutions, covering Shariah supervisory board composition, independence, and reporting.
- **Waqf administrator identity**: the institution managing the Waqf corpus is a distinct identity from the grant distributing program; both must be declared.
- **GFGP ARS 1651**: organizational capacity standard applicable to the institutional Zakat or Waqf administrator.
- **INPAS**: accounting standard governing restricted fund classification, applicable to Waqf corpus management and grant restriction documentation.

Compatibility statements: AAOIFI Governance Standards, GFGP ARS 1651, INPAS International Non-Profit Accounting Standard.
