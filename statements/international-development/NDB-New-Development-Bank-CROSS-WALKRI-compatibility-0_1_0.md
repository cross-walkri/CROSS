# New Development Bank (NDB) Evaluation Policy and Manual (2024) Compatibility - CROSS+WALKRI

## CROSS+WALKRI Compatibility Statement v0.1.0

---

## Summary

This statement documents structural alignment between the New Development Bank's 2024 evaluation documentation suite, comprising the Evaluation Policy (April 2024), Evaluation Manual (November 2024), IEO Corporate-Level Evaluation Policy Framework (October 2024), and IEO Strategy 2024-2026, and the CROSS+WALKRI specification standards. The NDB is the only major multilateral development bank with a predominantly non-Western governance structure, applying a co-ownership model with BRICS member states and expanded membership across Asia, Africa, Latin America, and the Middle East. CROSS satisfies NDB evaluation requirements as a consequence of OECD DAC and World Bank framework coverage, with additional structural alignment specific to NDB's member country ownership model and its freshly published 2024 documentation suite.

---

## The NDB Evaluation Framework

The New Development Bank was established in 2014 by Brazil, Russia, India, China, and South Africa under the BRICS framework. Its membership has since expanded to include Egypt, the United Arab Emirates, Bangladesh, and Uruguay, with additional membership in process. The NDB finances infrastructure and sustainable development projects in its member countries, which are emerging market and developing countries (EMDCs). Its governance model is built on co-ownership: each original BRICS member holds equal voting weight, and the Bank's operational mandate is explicitly oriented toward member country priorities rather than toward conditionality frameworks associated with Western-aligned multilateral development banks.

The NDB's 2024 evaluation documentation suite is the most current among multilateral development bank evaluation standards in CROSS+WALKRI's coverage. The Evaluation Policy (April 2024) establishes the Bank's commitment to independent evaluation, defines the mandate of the Independent Evaluation Office (IEO), and sets out the standards for evaluation quality and use. The Evaluation Manual (November 2024) provides operational guidance for evaluation design, data collection, and reporting, and is the primary document governing how evaluations of NDB-financed operations are conducted. The IEO Corporate-Level Evaluation Policy Framework (October 2024) governs portfolio-level and institutional evaluations. The IEO Strategy 2024-2026 sets evaluation priorities for the current period.

NDB's evaluation framework follows internationally accepted evaluation standards, informed by OECD DAC EvalNet criteria. The standard OECD DAC six criteria apply: relevance, coherence, effectiveness, efficiency, impact, and sustainability. The NDB results chain follows standard MDB practice: inputs, activities, outputs, outcomes, and impact. However, the NDB's institutional context introduces features that distinguish it from Western-aligned MDBs. First, member country ownership in evaluation design means that evaluation approaches are expected to reflect the priorities and contexts of borrowing countries, not impose standardized evaluation instruments developed elsewhere. Second, the IEO independence requirements reflect the Bank's commitment to evaluation credibility as a governance function, not merely a learning function. Third, the Bank operates without the structural adjustment conditionality that characterized earlier generations of MDB lending, which affects the types of outcomes that evaluation is designed to assess.

## How CROSS Satisfies the NDB Evaluation Framework

CROSS satisfies NDB evaluation requirements through two distinct pathways. The first is the consequence of OECD DAC coverage: because CROSS implements OECD DAC evaluation criteria structurally, and because NDB's framework is informed by OECD DAC EvalNet standards, CROSS's alignment with NDB evaluation requirements follows from that shared methodological foundation. The second pathway addresses features specific to the NDB's institutional context.

NDB's results chain (inputs, activities, outputs, outcomes, impact, NDB contribution) maps directly to CROSS's Theory of Change hierarchy. Inputs correspond to CROSS's organizational identity and resource declaration fields. Activities correspond to the CROSS activities tier. Outputs correspond to the CROSS Build obligation completion gate deliverable specification. Outcomes correspond to the CROSS Change obligation target condition and Fields 7-11. Impact corresponds to the CROSS goal-level declaration and population scope. NDB's additional results chain element, NDB contribution, corresponds to CROSS's coherence disclosure: the declaration of what specifically CROSS-structured program activities contributed to the outcome, as distinct from other actors and conditions.

NDB's emphasis on member country ownership in evaluation design corresponds to CROSS's coherence disclosure and population scope declaration. CROSS requires that every round specification declare the population whose conditions the program is designed to affect and situate program-level outcomes within broader population-level trends. For programs in EMDC contexts financed through NDB channels, this declaration must reflect the specific conditions, baselines, and trends in the member country context, not generic indicators. CROSS's population scope declaration is not a standardized instrument; it requires that the scope be declared in terms meaningful to the specific context, which is consistent with NDB's ownership model.

The IEO independence requirements correspond to CROSS's independent attestation pathway and conflict of interest provisions. CROSS Part VII requires that attestations of gate completion be made by parties without a direct financial interest in the outcome, and that attestation records be preserved in the Attestation Corpus independent of the attesting parties' own documentation. This is the structural form of the independence requirement that NDB's IEO mandate imposes at the institutional level.

The NDB's Evaluation Manual's data quality requirements correspond to WALKRI's pre-publication instrument specifications. The Manual requires that data collection instruments be designed to produce reliable and valid data, and that data quality be assessed as part of evaluation design. WALKRI's five pre-publication field requirements implement data quality at the instrument design stage, before any applicant or project team encounters the instrument.

|---|---|
| Results chain: inputs through impact | CROSS Theory of Change hierarchy; CROSS obligation mode selection |
| NDB contribution (additionality) | CROSS coherence disclosure |
| Member country ownership in evaluation design | CROSS population scope declaration; context-specific indicator specification |
| IEO independence standards | CROSS independent attestation pathway; Part VII conflict of interest provisions |
| Data quality requirements (Evaluation Manual) | WALKRI five pre-publication field requirements |

## How WALKRI Complements This Alignment

WALKRI ensures that intake fields for NDB-financed programs in EMDC contexts are measurement instruments before any application or program document is submitted. The NDB's Evaluation Manual's data quality expectations require that variables be operationally defined before data collection begins. WALKRI's pre-publication audit formalizes this requirement at the instrument design stage: every field must have its construct, metric, unit, baseline, target, and evidence threshold specified before the field is presented to any respondent. For programs in member country contexts, this means WALKRI specifications must reference country-specific baseline data sources and country-appropriate measurement units, consistent with the NDB's ownership model.

NDB's freshly published 2024 documentation suite represents the most current iteration of multilateral development bank evaluation practice among the standards in CROSS+WALKRI's coverage. The currency of the documentation makes this compatibility statement particularly useful for practitioners working at the intersection of MDB financing and CROSS+WALKRI implementation, because the requirements documented here reflect NDB's current operational standards rather than an older framework that may have been superseded.
