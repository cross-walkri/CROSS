# Global Affairs Canada (ODAAA, FIAP, and PMF)

## CROSS+WALKRI Compatibility Statement v0.1.0

---

## Summary

Canada's international development grant system operates under three overlapping frameworks: the Official Development Assistance Accountability Act (ODAAA), the Feminist International Assistance Policy (FIAP, 2017), and Global Affairs Canada's Results-Based Management system requiring Logic Models and Performance Measurement Frameworks (PMFs). Together these frameworks set substantive criteria for poverty reduction and human rights alignment, mandate gender equality integration assessments, and require specific planning document formats. CROSS satisfies all three structural layers through its gate architecture, Theory of Change hierarchy, and disaggregation ratchet; WALKRI supports FIAP's GE+ quality requirement at the measurement instrument level.

---

## The Global Affairs Canada Framework's Approach

Canadian international development assistance operates under a notably layered compliance structure. Unlike bilateral aid systems governed primarily by a single evaluation policy, Canadian ODA recipients must satisfy requirements originating from three distinct instruments simultaneously, each with different legislative or policy authority and each emphasizing different dimensions of program quality.

The Official Development Assistance Accountability Act (ODAAA) is federal legislation that constrains what can count as official development assistance for Canada. It sets three cumulative conditions: all Canadian ODA must contribute to poverty reduction, it must take into account the perspectives of the poor, and it must be consistent with international human rights standards. These are not procedural requirements about how programs are documented; they are substantive criteria about what programs are permitted to pursue. Any grant recipient whose program cannot demonstrate compliance across all three conditions is ineligible to receive ODA funding regardless of other merits.

The Feminist International Assistance Policy, adopted in 2017, added a mandatory Gender Equality Plus (GE+) assessment rating applied to every project. The GE+ system evaluates both the degree to which gender equality is integrated into the project's design and the capacity of the implementing partner for gender-based analysis. A GE+ rating is not a box to check at submission; it is an ongoing assessment that must be supportable across the program lifecycle. Programs that claim a GE+ rating at entry but cannot demonstrate how their measurement system tracks gender-disaggregated outcomes over time face rating downgrades and funding consequences.

Global Affairs Canada's Results-Based Management system adds a third layer of procedural requirements. All grant and contribution recipients must use prescribed Logic Model and PMF formats and submit them as part of their application. The Logic Model traces a standard activities-outputs-outcomes-goal hierarchy. The PMF translates that hierarchy into specific indicators with collection methods, baselines, and targets. Both documents must be submitted in Global Affairs Canada's formats, not in applicants' preferred alternatives. This creates a de facto standardization requirement for planning documents across the Canadian ODA recipient ecosystem.

## How CROSS Satisfies the Global Affairs Canada Framework

CROSS satisfies all three frameworks through distinct structural mechanisms at the appropriate level of each requirement.

**ODAAA compliance.** The ODAAA's poverty reduction criterion requires demonstrating who benefits and how their measurable condition improves through a traceable causal pathway. CROSS's population scope declaration, required at the entry gate, specifies the affected population with the precision needed to support this demonstration. CROSS's outcome declarations specify what measurable condition is expected to change. Together these fields constitute the structural form of a poverty reduction argument.

The "perspectives of the poor" requirement is more demanding than a beneficiary identification statement. It requires that program design demonstrate evidence drawn from or validated by affected communities rather than solely from program administrators. CROSS addresses this through the coherence disclosure and the independent evidence pathway within CROSS's evidence strength taxonomy: evidence sources documented as independent from the grantee organization provide the structural form of community-perspective evidence that ODAAA requires.

The human rights consistency requirement is addressed through CROSS's public benefit mechanism declaration, which requires programs to state the mechanism by which their intervention produces benefit for a population, traceable against recognized human rights frameworks.

**FIAP and GE+ compliance.** The GE+ rating system's longitudinal quality requirement is the most structurally demanding element for most programs to satisfy, because it requires not just gender disaggregation at a single point but consistent gender-disaggregated tracking across the program lifecycle. CROSS's disaggregation ratchet addresses this directly: once a gender disaggregation is established in a reporting period, it cannot be dropped in subsequent periods. This creates an architecturally enforced longitudinal gender-equity tracking mechanism rather than relying on program administrator discretion to maintain disaggregation across rounds.

**Logic Model and PMF compliance.** The Logic Model format is satisfied by the CROSS Theory of Change hierarchy. CROSS requires activities, outputs, outcomes, and goal-level declarations in sequence, which is the standard Logic Model structure. The PMF's required elements, including indicator definitions, collection methods, baselines, targets, and reporting frequency, are covered by CROSS's eleven-field indicator specification. Programs using CROSS complete the PMF's required elements as a byproduct of completing CROSS entry gate documentation, eliminating the need to maintain parallel documentation systems.

| Global Affairs Canada Requirement | CROSS Structural Element |
|---|---|
| ODAAA poverty reduction criterion | Population scope + outcome declarations with causal pathway |
| Perspectives of the poor | Independent evidence pathway in evidence strength taxonomy |
| Human rights consistency | Public benefit mechanism declaration |
| GE+ longitudinal rating | Disaggregation ratchet (gender disaggregation cannot be dropped) |
| Logic Model format | Theory of Change hierarchy (activities-outputs-outcomes-goal) |
| PMF required elements | Eleven-field indicator specification |

## How WALKRI Complements This Alignment

WALKRI's pre-publication field specification requirements address the GE+ assessment's data quality dimension. A GE+ rating depends not only on whether gender disaggregation is planned but on whether the measurement instruments collecting gender and equity data are specified precisely enough to produce reliable, comparable results across reporting periods. WALKRI requires that each intake field be specified with instrument type, collection method, verification pathway, and access information before applications open. For programs operating under FIAP's GE+ requirements, WALKRI-compliant field specifications for gender and equity data items constitute the measurement instrument documentation that supports a credible, sustained GE+ rating. This means WALKRI compliance and GE+ evidentiary quality reinforce each other directly: the instrument-level precision WALKRI requires is the same precision that makes gender-disaggregated data defensible across program cycles.
