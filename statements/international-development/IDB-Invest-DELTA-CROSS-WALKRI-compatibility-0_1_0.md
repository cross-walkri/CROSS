# IDB Invest DELTA Impact Rating System Compatibility

## CROSS+WALKRI Compatibility Statement v0.1.0

---

## Summary

IDB Invest, the Inter-American Development Bank's private sector window, applies its DELTA impact rating system to every investment in its portfolio, scoring each on a 0-10 scale across four analytical components: stakeholder analysis, additionality assessment, sustainability assessment, and economic and social rate-of-return approximation. CROSS's structural fields satisfy each DELTA component as a consequence of conformance, and the gate architecture produces annually updatable evidence that corresponds to DELTA's annual supervision requirement. WALKRI ensures that the indicator data feeding DELTA's rate-of-return approximation meets the specification standards that IDB Invest evaluators require.

DELTA is the primary impact measurement instrument for private sector development finance in Latin America and the Caribbean, applied across a portfolio exceeding fifteen billion USD. This compatibility statement is relevant for programs and funds seeking IDB Invest co-investment or technical assistance, and for regional grantmakers seeking credibility with IDB Invest's standards.

---

## What IDB Invest DELTA Is

IDB Invest (formally the Inter-American Investment Corporation) is the private sector arm of the Inter-American Development Bank Group. It provides loans, equity investments, guarantees, and technical assistance to private sector companies, financial intermediaries, and funds operating in Latin America and the Caribbean. Unlike the IDB's sovereign lending window, IDB Invest targets private sector actors and applies a distinct impact methodology calibrated for market-rate and below-market-rate private investment.

The DELTA impact rating system is embedded within IDB Invest's "Impact Management Framework," which describes the full lifecycle of impact assessment from pre-investment screening through annual supervision and exit evaluation. DELTA stands for Development Effectiveness in LAC Through Assessment. It is applied at the investment level before commitment and updated annually during portfolio supervision. The results feed the publicly reported Development Effectiveness Overview (DEO), which IDB Invest publishes annually.

DELTA scores each investment on four components:

**Stakeholder analysis**: Who is affected by the investment, how substantially, and through what pathway? This component assesses the breadth and depth of benefit to target populations, including unintended effects on non-target stakeholders.

**Additionality assessment**: What would have happened without the IDB Invest investment? This component asks whether the private sector actor would have undertaken the same activity on the same terms without IDB Invest's participation, and whether the impact claimed is genuinely additional to what market forces would have produced.

**Sustainability assessment**: Are the benefits durable? This component assesses whether the intervention produces outcomes that persist after the investment period, whether they are supported by viable institutional arrangements, and whether the financial model underpinning delivery is sound.

**Economic and social rate-of-return approximation**: What is the return on investment when social and environmental benefits are quantified and incorporated? This component requires pre-specified, baseline-anchored indicator data with clearly defined units, baselines, and targets.

The four components are scored and combined into a single DELTA rating (0-10). Investments below a threshold score are subject to enhanced supervision or excluded from the portfolio. The DELTA methodology is updated periodically and published at idbinvest.org.

## How CROSS Satisfies the DELTA Framework

CROSS's structural architecture addresses each of the four DELTA components through fields that programs must complete as gate conditions, rather than as optional supplementary documentation. This means a CROSS-conformant program produces DELTA-ready evidence as a structural byproduct of conformance, without any additional analytical effort.

**Stakeholder analysis.** DELTA's stakeholder analysis asks who is affected, how substantially, and with what distribution of benefits and harms across the affected population. CROSS addresses this through two fields: the population scope declaration, which specifies who is served (identity, geographic scope, demographic parameters, and scale), and the coherence disclosure, which requires the program to declare how it relates to other interventions operating in the same domain with the same population. The coherence disclosure is the CROSS instrument for capturing non-target stakeholder effects: programs must document whether their intervention displaces, complements, or substitutes for other services reaching the same population. Together, these two fields provide the evidence base for DELTA's stakeholder analysis component.

**Additionality assessment.** DELTA's additionality assessment is the component most distinctive to development finance: it asks whether the impact is genuinely additional, meaning whether it would have occurred without IDB Invest participation. CROSS addresses this through two fields. The causality stance field requires the program to declare whether it claims attribution (it is the primary cause of the outcome) or contribution (it is one of several causal factors). The additionality argument, which is a required element of the entry specification for programs claiming attribution, specifies the counterfactual basis: what would have happened in the absence of the intervention, and what evidence supports that counterfactual claim. This is precisely what DELTA's additionality assessors evaluate. A program that has completed the CROSS entry specification gate has already produced the additionality documentation that IDB Invest requires.

**Sustainability assessment.** DELTA's sustainability component asks whether benefits persist after the investment period and whether the delivery model is financially viable. CROSS addresses this through the continuation gate, which is the gate type associated with multi-year programs transitioning between phases. The continuation gate requires documentation of what was achieved in the preceding period, what institutional arrangements are in place to sustain the achievement, and how the next phase builds on completed work. For single-phase programs, CROSS's completion gate evidence includes a sustainability narrative as part of the completion determination. The continuation gate record is the most direct evidence available for DELTA's sustainability scoring.

**Economic and social rate-of-return approximation.** DELTA's rate-of-return approximation requires pre-specified, baseline-anchored indicator data with defined units, baselines, and targets for the key benefit streams. This is the component for which CROSS's eleven-field indicator specification is most directly designed. Fields 1 through 5 (indicator name, rationale, measurement form, operational definition, and construction methodology) establish what is being measured and how. Fields 10 and 11 (baseline and target) establish the pre-specified, baseline-anchored values that DELTA's rate-of-return calculation requires. Field 6 (cumulative or non-cumulative designation) resolves the aggregation question that frequently introduces ambiguity in rate-of-return calculations.

| DELTA Component | CROSS Provision |
|:--|:--|
| Stakeholder analysis | Population scope declaration; coherence disclosure |
| Additionality assessment | Causality stance field; additionality argument; counterfactual baseline specification |
| Sustainability assessment | Continuation gate evidence documentation; completion gate sustainability narrative |
| Rate-of-return approximation | Eleven-field indicator specification; Fields 1-5 (what is measured and how); Fields 10-11 (baseline and target) |

**Annual supervision alignment.** DELTA is updated annually during portfolio supervision. This means IDB Invest requires annually refreshed evidence for each scored component: updated stakeholder impact evidence, a reconfirmed additionality assessment, an updated sustainability read, and revised rate-of-return estimates based on reported actuals. CROSS's gate architecture produces exactly this cadence. A program operating annual progress verification gates produces an updated gate record each year, with refreshed indicator values, updated coherence context, and a current sustainability narrative. The CROSS annual gate record is the evidence document that DELTA's annual supervision update requires.

## How WALKRI Complements This Alignment

IDB Invest's DELTA rate-of-return approximation depends critically on the quality of indicator data: specifically, on whether baseline and target values were defined using the same operational methodology, whether reported actuals are comparable to baseline values, and whether the indicator's counting rule is sufficiently precise that different data collectors would produce consistent results. When indicator specifications are ambiguous, the rate-of-return approximation becomes unreliable, and DELTA's analytical credibility is undermined.

WALKRI addresses this at the collection instrument level. By requiring each data collection field to specify its criterion intent, operational definition, construction methodology, and evidence form before any program operator or beneficiary submits data, WALKRI ensures that the values feeding DELTA's rate-of-return calculation are produced by pre-specified methodologies that a DELTA evaluator can verify. The WALKRI Accuracy criterion directly addresses the measurement comparability problem that rate-of-return calculations require: it specifies whether the field measures what it claims to measure, using a methodology that would produce consistent results across different data collectors and time periods.

For programs seeking IDB Invest co-investment or technical assistance in the Latin America and Caribbean region, WALKRI conformance provides a competitive advantage in the pre-investment DELTA assessment: the program can demonstrate, through its WALKRI field specifications, that its impact data meets the specification quality standards that IDB Invest evaluators require. This shifts the DELTA pre-investment review from a data quality investigation to a structured specification verification, which is faster and more likely to produce a high DELTA score.
