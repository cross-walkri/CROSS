# DCED Standard for Results Measurement Compatibility

## CROSS+WALKRI Compatibility Statement v0.1.0

---

## Summary

CROSS's Theory of Change architecture and eleven-field indicator specification satisfy five of the seven DCED Standard elements directly. The remaining two elements address market-systems and poverty measurement, which are substantive program design requirements rather than documentation or architecture requirements; CROSS supports rather than substitutes for them.

The Donor Committee for Enterprise Development Standard for Results Measurement is used by more than 100 programs across 50-plus countries. Its 22 member agencies include USAID, FCDO, SDC, GIZ, and SIDA. IRIS+ explicitly recognizes DCED as an interoperable framework through a joint interoperability document. Because CROSS already references IRIS+ as a compatible standard, DCED alignment is a direct extension of that existing compatibility: programs that satisfy CROSS under IRIS+ alignment satisfy DCED elements through the same structural provisions.

---

## What the DCED Standard Requires

The DCED Standard specifies seven elements for results measurement in market systems and enterprise development programs:

1. Articulate the Theory of Change
2. Plan data collection on changes in the target group
3. Measure changes in the wider market or system
4. Collect data on the program's contribution to observed changes
5. Measure poverty and socioeconomic impacts on target populations
6. Manage the complexity of attributing results to the program
7. Use performance data to improve program delivery

These elements are particularly relevant for programs operating at ecosystem or market-systems scale, including grant rounds in public goods and regenerative finance ecosystems. At that scale, the distinction between Element 3 (wider system change) and Elements 2 and 5 (target group change) becomes analytically significant and requires explicit design choices in the measurement architecture.

## CROSS+WALKRI Mapping

| DCED Element | CROSS/WALKRI Provision | Assessment |
| :-- | :-- | :-- |
| 1. Articulate the Theory of Change | Program-level ToC declaration (Goal, Outcome, Output, Activity hierarchy; CROSS Part IX-B) | Full satisfaction. CROSS requires an explicit ToC declaration at the program level with pathway registry. The four-level hierarchy (Goal, Outcome, Output, Activity) corresponds to the DCED's ToC requirements. |
| 2. Plan data collection on changes in target group | Eleven-field indicator specification, particularly Fields 6 (population), 8 (data source), 9 (frequency), and 10 (responsible party) | Full satisfaction. CROSS Fields 6 through 10 collectively specify who will collect data on which population, using which method, at which intervals, with which organizational accountability. |
| 3. Measure changes in the wider market or system | ToC pathway registry and Output-level indicators for systemic change | Partial satisfaction. CROSS's ToC architecture accommodates systemic and market-level indicators, but programs must explicitly declare system-level pathways in their ToC and design Output-level indicators that capture systemic change. CROSS enables this design; it does not mandate it. |
| 4. Collect data on the program's contribution | Causality stance (Indicator Field 11); gate evidence requirements | Full satisfaction. CROSS Field 11 (causality stance) requires programs to declare whether they are claiming attribution or contribution for each indicator. The contribution stance configures evidence requirements to capture the program's role relative to co-factors, which is the core of DCED Element 4. |
| 5. Measure poverty and socioeconomic impacts | Population field (Indicator Field 6); IRIS+ alignment in Field 10 | Partial satisfaction. CROSS requires population specification and supports IRIS+ socioeconomic indicator categories. Programs must design indicators at the poverty and socioeconomic level explicitly; CROSS provides the architecture but does not mandate poverty measurement. |
| 6. Manage the complexity of attributing results | Causality stance (Indicator Field 11); gate character (developmental vs. summative) | Full satisfaction. CROSS's causality stance field directly addresses attribution complexity. Contribution stance acknowledges co-factors; attribution stance requires counterfactual evidence. The developmental gate character supports adaptive inference as evidence accumulates. This is the most direct structural match between CROSS and the DCED Standard. |
| 7. Use performance data to improve program delivery | Developmental gate character; progress verification gate outputs; continuation gate sustainability stance | Full satisfaction. CROSS's developmental gate architecture requires that progress verification gates surface what needs to change, not merely whether targets were met. The continuation gate's three-position sustainability stance (sustained, conditional, dependent) generates adaptive evidence for program improvement decisions. |

**Result:** CROSS fully satisfies DCED Elements 1, 2, 4, 6, and 7 as structural consequences of conformance. CROSS partially satisfies Elements 3 and 5; these require deliberate program design choices that CROSS supports but does not mandate.

## The IRIS+ Bridge

IRIS+ and the DCED Standard are recognized as interoperable through a joint document published by the Global Impact Investing Network. That document establishes explicit crosswalks between IRIS+ metrics and DCED data collection requirements, particularly for enterprise development and market-systems programs.

CROSS already references IRIS+ as a compatible standard. Programs that declare IRIS+ alignment in Indicator Field 10 (institutional framework alignment) produce IRIS+-compatible indicator specifications. Because IRIS+ and DCED are interoperable, those same indicator specifications satisfy DCED data collection requirements. The alignment is not circular: CROSS, IRIS+, and DCED address complementary layers. CROSS specifies the obligation architecture and indicator specification quality; IRIS+ provides the metric taxonomy; DCED provides the results measurement process standard.

## Ecosystem-Scale Application

The DCED Standard's emphasis on market and system-level change is especially relevant for grant rounds operating at ecosystem scale. In regenerative finance, public goods, and impact DAO contexts, a single grant round may allocate resources across dozens of projects contributing to a shared ecosystem outcome. At that scale, Element 3 (wider system change) and Element 6 (attribution complexity) are not peripheral concerns; they are central to the measurement design.

CROSS's program-level ToC pathway registry supports this use case. Programs can declare multiple pathways from Activities through Outputs and Outcomes to the shared Goal, making the contribution logic explicit for each funded project within the ecosystem round. The causality stance field allows each pathway to carry its own attribution or contribution claim, accommodating the mixed-method evidence landscape that ecosystem-scale programs typically produce.

For programs using CROSS at the grant round level, configuring the activation gate to capture ecosystem baseline conditions and the progress verification gates to track systemic indicators alongside grantee-level outputs satisfies the DCED distinction between target-group change and wider-system change.

## Adoption Guidance

Programs using the DCED Standard that adopt CROSS+WALKRI should:

1. Declare IRIS+ alignment in Indicator Field 10 for all indicators that correspond to IRIS+ metric categories. This activates the IRIS+/DCED interoperability pathway and ensures that CROSS indicator specifications satisfy DCED data collection requirements under the joint interoperability framework.

2. Explicitly design system-level indicators in the ToC pathway registry. DCED Element 3 requires measurement of change in the wider market or system, not only in the target group. These indicators should appear at the Outcome level of the CROSS ToC with Field 6 (population) scoped to the system level rather than the direct beneficiary population.

3. Declare contribution stance in Indicator Field 11 for all program indicators unless the program has a randomized or quasi-experimental design that justifies attribution stance. Contribution stance is the default appropriate to DCED's element 6 requirements for most enterprise development and ecosystem programs.

4. Configure progress verification gates with developmental character. DCED Element 7 requires that performance data be used to improve delivery; developmental gates generate the learning outputs this element requires at each reporting milestone.

Programs reporting to DCED Standard auditors do not need to produce separate results measurement documentation beyond what CROSS conformance produces for Elements 1, 2, 4, 6, and 7. Documentation for Elements 3 and 5 depends on indicator design choices the program makes within the CROSS architecture.
