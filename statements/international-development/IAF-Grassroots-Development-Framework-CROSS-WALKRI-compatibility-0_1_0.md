# Inter-American Foundation Grassroots Development Framework

## CROSS+WALKRI Compatibility Statement v0.1.0

---

## Summary

The Inter-American Foundation (IAF) is an independent US federal agency that funds grassroots development in Latin America and the Caribbean. Its Grassroots Development Framework (GDF) is a 41-indicator participatory results system organized across three levels: individual and household, grantee organization, and community and society. Grantees select applicable indicators from the menu at the start of each grant and establish baselines with IAF assistance before activities begin. CROSS+WALKRI satisfies the GDF's measurement requirements structurally, with CROSS's entry specification gate accommodating the GDF's participatory indicator selection model and WALKRI's pre-publication requirements addressing the primary data quality risk: that indicator selection is participatory but operational definitions of what evidence constitutes a valid data point are not standardized across the grantee portfolio. Note: users of this compatibility statement should verify IAF's current operational status given federal agency reorganizations in 2025.

---

## The IAF Grassroots Development Framework

The GDF is the IAF's core results measurement system, developed over decades of practice in community-level development funding across Latin America and the Caribbean. The framework organizes 41 indicators across three result levels. The individual and household level captures changes in income, employment, health access, housing, food security, and similar tangible conditions for the people directly served by IAF-funded organizations. The grantee organization level captures changes in the capacity, governance, financial sustainability, and leadership of the organizations receiving IAF grants. The community and society level captures changes in local and national policy environments, social cohesion, civic participation, and collective action capacity.

A distinctive feature of the GDF is participatory indicator selection. Unlike WIOA or Perkins V, which fix indicators by regulation, or SNAP-Ed, which fixes priority indicators by FNS requirement, the GDF presents a menu of 41 indicators and allows grantees to select the indicators applicable to their context, with IAF confirmation of the selection before the grant begins. This participatory approach reflects the IAF's founding commitment to grassroots agency: the organizations that know their communities best are positioned to identify which indicators are meaningful for their work. Grantees establish baselines for their selected indicators with IAF technical assistance before any activities begin, and they report at six-month intervals throughout the grant period.

The GDF covers both tangible and intangible results. Tangible results (income change, employment rate, health service access) are measured against quantitative baselines using field instruments. Intangible results (organizational capacity, social cohesion, policy influence) are measured using qualitative instruments, narrative evidence, and structured self-assessment. GDF data is reported to the IAF board, OMB, and Congress under the Government Performance and Results Act (GPRA), making the framework part of the US federal performance reporting architecture.

## How CROSS Satisfies the IAF Grassroots Development Framework

CROSS addresses the GDF's measurement requirements across its participatory indicator selection model, its three-level results hierarchy, and its baseline data requirement.

| GDF Element | CROSS Provision |
|:--|:--|
| 41-indicator menu, participatory selection before grant begins | Entry specification gate: indicator selection committed to before any activity begins; CROSS accommodates grantee-selected indicators within a fixed menu |
| IAF confirmation of indicator selection | Entry specification gate: the IAF confirmation process corresponds to the gate's review function |
| Baseline data established with IAF assistance before activities begin | Field 4 (baseline): counterfactual starting point established before the intervention begins |
| Individual and household result level | Outcome tier: changes in the condition of people who received program services |
| Grantee organization result level | Output tier: organizational capacity as a measurable intermediate result of grant activities |
| Community and society result level | Goal tier: community and societal conditions to which individual and organizational outcomes contribute |
| Six-month reporting intervals | Field 6 (timing): reporting cadence specified at the entry specification gate |
| Tangible results (income, employment, health access) | Fields 1-5 (name, definition, unit, baseline, target) + Field 9 (collection method: field instruments with quantitative protocols) |
| Intangible results (organizational capacity, social cohesion) | Fields 1-5 + Field 10 (causality stance) + Field 11 (evidence threshold: narrative evidence standards specified before data collection begins) |
| GPRA reporting requirements | Field 8 (data source): administrative data sources specified to satisfy GPRA reporting chains |
| Portfolio reporting to IAF board, OMB, Congress | Portfolio aggregation: grantee-level outcomes aggregate to portfolio-level GDF reporting |

The GDF's participatory indicator selection model is a distinct form of CROSS's entry specification gate. In fixed-indicator frameworks like WIOA or Perkins V, the gate function is primarily a confirmation that statutory or regulatory indicators have been correctly specified with all required fields. In the GDF, the gate function includes a prior step: the selection of which indicators from the 41-item menu are applicable to the specific grantee and context. CROSS accommodates this by treating the IAF's indicator menu as the published constraint within which the entry specification gate operates. A GDF grantee that is CROSS-conformant selects its indicators from the menu before any activity begins, specifies all eleven fields for each selected indicator, and receives IAF confirmation before the grant start date. The gate is satisfied when the confirmed indicator specification exists in writing before the first activity is implemented.

The three-level GDF structure maps directly to CROSS's Theory of Change hierarchy, with a specificity that is unusual among development frameworks. Individual and household outcomes correspond to CROSS's outcome tier: these are changes in the condition of the people whom IAF-funded organizations directly serve, measured at intervals against a pre-established baseline. Grantee organization outcomes correspond to CROSS's output tier: organizational capacity, governance quality, and financial sustainability are intermediate results that the grant activities are designed to produce, and that in turn enable the organization to produce individual and community outcomes. Community and society outcomes correspond to CROSS's goal tier: the long-term conditions at the community and societal level to which individual and organizational improvements are intended to contribute over time.

The GDF's baseline data requirement is the framework's most direct operationalization of a CROSS field. Field 4 of the eleven-field indicator specification requires a baseline: the measured starting condition against which change will be assessed. The GDF's requirement that baselines be established with IAF technical assistance before any activities begin means that a GDF-conformant grantee has already satisfied Field 4 as a structural consequence of GDF compliance.

## How WALKRI Complements This Alignment

WALKRI's pre-publication requirements address the GDF's primary data quality risk, which the participatory selection model creates by design. The 41-indicator menu provides indicator labels and brief definitions for each indicator, but it does not standardize the operational definition of what evidence a field worker must observe before coding a positive outcome for any given indicator. Different grantees interpreting the same indicator label may use different observation protocols, different documentation standards, and different threshold criteria, producing indicator data that appears comparable across the IAF portfolio (because the same indicator labels are reported) but is not commensurable in practice.

WALKRI's five requirements, applied to GDF intake and data collection instruments before the grant start date, resolve this at source. The criterion intent requirement ensures that the grantee articulates, in writing and before data collection begins, what the selected indicator is designed to detect and at what level of the GDF's three-tier hierarchy it operates. This prevents scope drift in which an organizational capacity indicator is operationalized to capture individual behavior change, or a community outcome indicator is measured through a single household survey. The operational definition requirement specifies precisely what an observed condition must look like to qualify as a positive data point: not the indicator's general label but the specific observable characteristics the field instrument is designed to detect. The response form requirement specifies the format of the instrument through which data is collected: a structured interview guide, a participatory self-assessment form, a community mapping protocol, or a financial record review checklist. The evidence form requirement specifies what documentation must accompany a positive outcome record. The compliance threshold specifies the minimum standard a data point must meet before it is entered into the six-month GDF report.

Applied to the GDF's intangible indicators, WALKRI's requirements are especially consequential. Social cohesion and policy influence indicators are the most contextually variable in the framework: what constitutes evidence of improved social cohesion in a rural Andean cooperative differs from what constitutes evidence of improved social cohesion in an urban Caribbean neighborhood organization. WALKRI's pre-publication requirements do not eliminate this contextual variation; they ensure that the contextually appropriate definition is committed to in writing before data collection begins, which is the minimum condition for the evidence to be auditable, comparable within the grantee's own reporting, and useful to the IAF's portfolio-level aggregation.
