# SNAP-Ed Evaluation Framework

## CROSS+WALKRI Compatibility Statement v0.1.0

---

## Summary

The USDA Food and Nutrition Service (FNS) Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program Education (SNAP-Ed) Evaluation Framework is a 51-indicator multi-level logic model that governs nutrition education and obesity prevention programming funded through SNAP-Ed. FNS requires states to submit annual SNAP-Ed Plans specifying how they will measure outcomes against the framework before funding is released. CROSS+WALKRI satisfies the framework's evaluation requirements structurally: the framework's four ecological levels map to CROSS's Theory of Change hierarchy, the annual Plan review serves as CROSS's entry specification gate at the state level, and WALKRI's pre-publication requirements address the primary data quality gap created when state agencies operationalize the framework's 51 indicator labels without standardized specification requirements.

---

## The SNAP-Ed Evaluation Framework

The SNAP-Ed Evaluation Framework, published by USDA FNS, organizes nutrition education and obesity prevention outcome measurement across four ecological levels: individual, environmental settings, sectors of influence, and population results. The framework's 51 outcome indicators span this ecological range, from individual dietary behavior change to population-level food security metrics. FNS requires states to measure a specified set of priority indicators across the ecological levels: MT1, MT2, MT3, and MT5 (medium-term behavioral and environmental outcomes), ST7 and ST8 (partnerships and coalition indicators), and R2 (fruit and vegetable consumption at the population level).

States submit annual SNAP-Ed Plans to FNS specifying how they will implement programming and measure outcomes against the framework. FNS reviews and approves these plans before funding is released, creating a formal pre-funding review gate. The 2024 update to the Behavior Outcome Measurement Toolkit added an explicit equity lens requirement, specifying that outcome measurement must address disparities by race, ethnicity, income, and geography. The Behavior Outcome Measurement Toolkit supplements the framework with validated instruments for measuring selected indicators, but instrument adoption is not universally required: states retain discretion to use alternative instruments if they can demonstrate validity.

The framework's priority indicator requirements apply at the state level, but delivery occurs through a mix of state-level interventions, community partnerships, and school-based programming, producing a portfolio of local activities that must aggregate to state-level outcome evidence. This multi-level delivery structure makes pre-specification of measurement plans especially consequential: without indicator specification committed to before programming begins, the data collected at community and school levels may not aggregate to the state priority indicators that FNS requires.

## How CROSS Satisfies the SNAP-Ed Evaluation Framework

CROSS addresses the SNAP-Ed Evaluation Framework's measurement requirements at both the structural gate level and the field-specification level.

| SNAP-Ed Framework Element | CROSS Provision |
|:--|:--|
| 51-indicator, four-ecological-level logic model | Theory of Change hierarchy: activities, outputs, outcomes, goals mapped across ecological levels |
| Priority indicator set (MT1, MT2, MT3, MT5, ST7, ST8, R2) required by FNS | Entry specification gate: priority indicators pre-specified before any state Plan is submitted |
| Annual Plan reviewed and approved by FNS before funding | Entry specification gate: evaluation structure published and approved before any programming begins |
| Individual behavior change indicators (MT tier) | Outcome tier of Theory of Change hierarchy |
| Environmental policy and systems change indicators | Outcome tier (policy and environmental outcomes distinguished from individual behavior outcomes) |
| Partnership and coalition indicators (ST7, ST8) | Output tier: coalition formation as a measurable intermediate output |
| Population-level food security metrics (R2) | Goal tier: population condition to which individual and environmental outcomes contribute |
| Equity lens requirement (2024 update) | Disaggregation ratchet: demographic disaggregation by race, ethnicity, income, geography established at Plan approval and maintained across reporting periods |
| Baseline data requirement | Field 4 (baseline): counterfactual starting point established before programming begins |
| State Plan specifies measurement approach for each indicator | Fields 1-9 (indicator specification): state Plan constitutes the entry specification gate document |

The SNAP-Ed framework's 51-indicator, four-ecological-level structure is a published pre-specification of outcome measurement domains that states must address before any nutrition education activity begins. The annual Plan review functions as CROSS's entry specification gate at the state level: a state SNAP-Ed Plan that is CROSS-conformant specifies each priority indicator with all eleven fields before FNS approves the plan and before any programming dollar is spent.

The ecological hierarchy maps directly to CROSS's Theory of Change hierarchy. Individual dietary behavior change outcomes (MT tier indicators) correspond to CROSS's outcome tier: near-to-medium-term changes in the condition of people who received program services. Environmental and policy change outcomes (systems-level SNAP-Ed indicators) correspond to CROSS's outcome tier at the environmental level: changes in the settings and conditions within which individuals make food choices. Population-level food security and consumption metrics (R2 and population result indicators) correspond to CROSS's goal tier: the long-term population condition to which the full portfolio of individual and environmental outcomes is intended to contribute. SNAP-Ed's partnership and coalition indicators (ST7, ST8) correspond to CROSS's output tier: coalition formation is a measurable intermediate result of program activities, prior to and distinct from the behavioral and environmental outcomes those coalitions are designed to produce.

The 2024 equity lens requirement operationalizes CROSS's disaggregation ratchet at the regulatory level. FNS now requires that outcome measurement address disparities by race, ethnicity, income, and geography. A CROSS-conformant state SNAP-Ed Plan establishes these demographic dimensions at the entry specification gate and cannot remove them in subsequent plan years or mid-year reporting revisions, which is exactly the structural protection the disaggregation ratchet provides.

## How WALKRI Complements This Alignment

WALKRI's pre-publication requirements address a structural gap that the SNAP-Ed framework creates by design. The framework specifies 51 indicator labels organized in a logic model structure, and FNS specifies which priority indicators states must measure. What the framework does not standardize is the operational definition of what evidence instrument will be used to measure each indicator, what response format is required, and what evidence threshold a data point must meet to count. These determinations are left to state implementing agencies, which means that MT1 data collected in California and MT1 data collected in Texas may reflect different operational definitions of the same indicator label.

WALKRI's five pre-publication requirements, applied to state SNAP-Ed Plan development before FNS approval, resolve this at source. The criterion intent requirement ensures that the purpose of each priority indicator is stated in terms that connect the indicator to the ecological level it is designed to measure. The operational definition requirement specifies what an observed behavior or environmental condition must look like to qualify as a positive outcome for each indicator. The response form requirement specifies the format of the instrument through which data is collected: a direct observation protocol, a validated dietary recall instrument, or a policy environment scan form. The evidence form requirement specifies what documentation must accompany a positive outcome coding. The compliance threshold requirement specifies the minimum evidentiary standard a data point must meet before it is entered into state reporting.

Applied to the Behavior Outcome Measurement Toolkit's validated instruments, WALKRI's requirements confirm that the toolkit instruments satisfy all five pre-publication requirements before a state adopts them. Applied to state-developed alternative instruments (which FNS permits where states demonstrate validity), WALKRI's requirements produce instrument specifications that are comparable across states at the definition level even where different data collection instruments are used. The result is a SNAP-Ed measurement system where the same indicator label reliably reflects the same underlying construct across state implementations.
